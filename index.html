What is sleep apnea?

Sleep apnea is when you stop breathing while asleep, due to a physiological obstruction to your airway. A sleep study, in which a physician monitors your vital signs while you sleep, is performed to determine if you have sleep apnea. If you stop breathing for at least 10 seconds at a time for 5 times per hour, then you would be diagnosed with sleep apnea.

More than 18 million Americans suffer from sleep apnea and many are undiagnosed. With this site, we hope to help you gain a better understanding of this disease, its health ramifications, and some treatment options. LEARN MORE

Here are a few treatment options for sleep apnea

Positional Change

Sleeping on your side my help improve sleep quality. Avoid sleeping on your back.

Weight Loss

People who are overweight or obese have extra tissue around their necks that can collapse the airway and block airflow to the lungs during sleep. Weight loss has been shown to greatly improve and potentially eliminate sleep apnea in people who are obese

Avoid Alcohol

Alcohol is a respiratory depressant and can exacerbate apnic spells.

Avoid Sedatives

Like alcohol, sedatives also act as respiratory depressants and can exacerbate apnic spells.

Dental Devices

Can relieve oropharynx obstruction by repositioning the jaw. Dental devices are good for: 1. people with mild to moderate sleep apnea who prefer this to CPAP machines, 2. people with mild to moderate sleep apnea who do not respond to CPAP. They are not effective for those with severe sleep apnea.

CPAP

CPAP is a machine that delivers pressurized air to your nose and throat to keep the airway open, preventing the stops in breathing while you sleep.

Surgery

Surgery can reshape or reposition upper airway structures, including bone and soft tissue.
